# engineering-leadership
Engineering Leadership Notes / Training

Becoming an Engineering Manager is a significant career inflection point in the life of an engineer and requires learning a new set of skills. Rather than being the expert in a particular language, framework, best practice or architecture pattern, you have to shift your thinking and focus on achieving goals by building and leading a healthy team. Your new performance metrics aren’t sprint commitments or code quality; they now also include the career growth of your team members, roadmapping, and healthy delivery outcomes.

Over the next 13 sessions, we’ll tackle some of the key skills necessary as an Engineering Manager. You’ll likely have had exposure to some of these, and some of them may be brand new. In each session, we will provide a tool and resources. The list of books on your reading list will likely pile up, but don’t worry. In our 1:1s we’ll discuss the areas to focus on most.

*Leadership is a journey. There is always time for more books.*
