Cross-Functional 1:1s

Cross-functional 1:1s (one-on-ones) are meetings between individuals from different functional areas within an organization. These meetings are an opportunity for team members to discuss collaboration, share updates, and address any challenges or opportunities that span multiple departments.

Meeting Agenda

1

Check-In

5 minutes

Brief personal updates to build rapport.

Example: "How has your week been so far?"

2

Project/Initiative Updates

15 minutes
  • Share updates on ongoing projects or initiatives that involve both functional areas
  • Discuss progress, challenges, and any adjustments needed

Example: "Let's discuss the status of the joint project between our teams."

3

Collaboration Opportunities

10 minutes
  • Identify potential areas for collaboration or joint efforts
  • Discuss how the teams can support each other's goals

Example: "Are there any upcoming projects where our teams can collaborate more closely?"

4

Communication and Feedback

15 minutes
  • Discuss communication channels and preferences
  • Share feedback on past collaborations and ways to improve

Example: "How can we improve communication between our teams?"

5

Upcoming Initiatives and Challenges

10 minutes
  • Highlight any upcoming initiatives or challenges on the horizon
  • Discuss potential impacts on both functional areas

Example: "I wanted to make you aware of an upcoming initiative that might affect your team."

6

Cross-Functional Support

10 minutes
  • Explore ways each team can provide support to the other
  • Discuss resources, expertise, or assistance that may be needed

Example: "Is there anything your team needs support with from our side?"

7

Action Items and Follow-Up

10 minutes
  • Summarize key takeaways and action items
  • Assign responsibilities for any follow-up tasks

Example: "Let's ensure we follow up on these action items before our next meeting."

8

Closing and Next Steps

5 minutes
  • Confirm key points discussed and any decisions made
  • Schedule the next cross-functional 1:1 if needed

Example: "Thank you for the productive discussion. Let's reconvene next week to follow up on these action items."

Additional Notes

  • • Keep the tone collaborative and open
  • • Encourage both parties to bring forward any concerns or ideas
  • • Adapt the agenda based on the specific needs of the cross-functional collaboration

Meeting Objective

The goal of these cross-functional 1:1s is to foster communication, build relationships, and ensure smooth collaboration between different functional areas within the organization. Adjust the agenda as needed to suit your team's dynamics and priorities.

Total Meeting Time: 80 minutes
