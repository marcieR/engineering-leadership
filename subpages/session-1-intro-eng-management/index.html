Dichotomy of Leadership

Leadership requires constant balance between opposing forces

Too much

Team feels overwhelmed — there's too much to do

⟷
Too little

Team slows down without a clear vision

No plan

Team doesn't know how to execute

⟷
Too much planning

Team won't execute without approval

Too close

Proximity becomes an impediment to honest feedback

⟷
Too distant

You lose the trust needed to lead effectively

Over-ownership

Team only executes at the boss's instruction

⟷
Under-ownership

No accountability; work drifts without a holder

Core Principle

Balance must be evaluated continuously and modulated for specific needs. A team member who falls behind needs closer management — once they're back on track, ease off.

Doing small things well

Builds trust — when it matters, your pushback lands.

Solid cross-functional relationships

Treat every department with mutual respect.

Humble enough to accept feedback

But hold the line when it risks the larger mission. A bad plan is better unexecuted.

Focused but detached

Step back from the details to see the bigger picture.

Feedback Models

SBI and COIN — two frameworks for clarity

Feedback types: reinforcing (keep doing this) vs. constructive (change this).

SBI Framework

S

Situation — the context in which it occurred

B

Behavior — the specific observable action

I

Impact — the effect on people or work

COIN Framework

C

Context — the situation / setting

O

Observation — objective facts, not perceptions

I

Impact — consequences for team or outcomes

N

Next Steps — what should change or continue

Principles

Feedback must be actionable, kind, and specific. Build a culture by modeling it, making it frequent, and normalizing the act of receiving it without defensiveness.

Overcoming Adversity with Cross-Functional Partners

The "Five Whys of Objection" method

When someone pushes back, diagnose the real reason before responding. Tailor your strategy to match the actual blocker.

01

They don't understand → educate

02

They don't agree → align on facts

03

They don't care → connect to their priorities

04

They don't believe it will work → prove with evidence

05

They feel threatened or overwhelmed → reduce the ask

Overcoming Adversity with Executive Comms

Stakeholder Mapping + Influence Strategy

Map stakeholders by Power (decision-making authority) and Interest (how much they care). Then tailor your approach accordingly.

High Power · High Interest

Co-create the plan

These are your most critical relationships — involve them early.

High Power · Low Interest

Speak in business outcomes

Lead with impact; keep it brief.

Low Power · High Interest

Use as advocates

They care and can amplify your message upward.

Low Power · Low Interest

Inform

Keep in the loop; don't over-invest.

Key Insight

This ensures you're using energy where it matters — not treating every stakeholder the same way.
